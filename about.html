Hi — I'm the founder of Talcott Ridge.

I applied to medical school in the post-COVID era and secured 9 interviews and 4 MD acceptances. I'm now a first-year medical student at Virginia Tech Carilion School of Medicine, where I'm also an HPSP scholar.

I started Talcott Ridge because the advising industry has a structural problem: the best-resourced students get the most help, and the cycle compounds from there. I went through this process without a consultant, figured out what worked through research and iteration, and I'd rather share that knowledge openly than put it behind a $5,000 paywall.

My academic background is in sociology, with a research focus on social networks and food insecurity. That training shapes how I approach advising — I'm interested in the structural context of your application, not just the surface-level polish.

I'm also a UConn graduate, which means I have particular familiarity with the northeastern premed pipeline and the specific pressures that come with it.

Perspective

What I can and can't offer

✅

What I bring

Recent cycle experience (post-COVID, post-reform). Familiarity with current AMCAS nuances. Honest feedback without a financial incentive to tell you what you want to hear. Genuine interest in access over exclusivity.

⚠️

What I can't offer

Guaranteed outcomes. Admissions committee insider knowledge. The bandwidth of a full-time firm. Unlimited availability — I'm a medical student first, and this practice reflects that honestly.

🧭

My advising philosophy

The best application is an honest one. My job isn't to make you sound like someone else — it's to help you articulate clearly who you already are and why that matters to medicine.
